Uchi


Uchi is a renowned Japanese restaurant. It was opened in 2003 by chef
Tyson Cole, who had previously trained in traditional Japanese cuisine
in Tokyo. Uchi offers a unique dining experience that combines traditional
Japanese flavors with contemporary techniques and locally-sourced ingredients.
The restaurant's menu features a wide range of sushi, sashimi, and other
small plates, as well as an extensive sake selection. Over the years, Uchi
has received numerous accolades and awards, including a James Beard Foundation
Award for Best Chef Southwest in 2011.

Benihana is a Japanese restaurant chain that was founded in 1964 in New York
City by Hiroaki "Rocky" Aoki. The restaurant is known for its teppanyaki-style
dining, where chefs prepare and cook food on a flat iron griddle in front of
customers. Benihana's menu features a variety of Japanese dishes, including sushi,
tempura, and teriyaki, but the teppanyaki-style dining is the restaurant's main
attraction. The chefs at Benihana are not only skilled at cooking food to perfection,
but they also put on a show for guests with their knife skills, flipping shrimp
tails into their hats, and making volcano-shaped onion rings that erupt in flames.
